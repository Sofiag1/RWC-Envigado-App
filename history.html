Our history

  • In summer 2007, RWC students were invited to present their real world projects at News12 New JerseyTV station and were interviewed by Brian Jenkins for more than 20 minutes.

  • In summer 2008, RWC students were invited to present their Global Microscope project to NASA headquarters in Washington DC and received an appreciation letter from NASA executive director of education.

  • RWC program was featured at least three times in the Star-Ledger, one time in the Daily Record, one time in Asbury Park Press and one time in NJN TV. The program was featured three years in a row in NJIT’s president annual report;.

  • All RWC showcases were under the patronage of NJIT’s provosts with big attendance of major corporations and numerous small businesses.

  • RWC offered satellite locations in Newark, Mount Olive and Freehold. Since 2008, RWC partnered with Newark Public Schools and recently with Orange school district.

  • RWC partnered with several departments at NJIT including computer science, math, bio medical engineering and school of architecture

  • The program partnerships reached Michigan and Puerto Rico with satellite programs in 2010 and 2011.
